About Our Library

Library has a good & rare collection of books, journals and magazines for students and staff members. Presently, above 43 thousand books, 69 national journals & magazines, and CDs/DVDs and back issues of journals are in the library.

The library is automated with an online portal accessible to all users. It runs on PSG-ILMS, an integrated Library Management System that supports all housekeeping operations. The Digital Library has 21 computers with high-speed internet for academic purposes.

About Us

Our Library has a rich collection of books and journals catering to students and faculty members. Presently, over 43 thousand books, 449 CDs/DVDs and 69 printed journals are possessed by the library.

The library is automated having an online portal that can be easily accessed by the users. The library implemented PSG-ILMS, an integrated Library Management System that supports all housekeeping operations of the library.

There is a separate Digital Library having 21 computers running on high-speed internet. The library system and staff is meticulously focused on providing a comfortable, user-friendly environment that enables learning and knowledge creation.

Home › Genesis & Growth

Genesis & Growth

1939 — The Library came into being with the establishment of PSG Polytechnic.
1956 — Further expanded with the genesis of PSG College of Technology.
With recurring grants from the Government, generous Management contribution, and special interest taken by Dr. G. R. Damodaran, the Library was built up and equipped steadily and systematically.
3rd December 2014 — A separate library was created for PSG Polytechnic College in I Block, 4th Floor.
The library currently holds resources above 43 thousand books.

Home › Rules & Regulations

General

  • Books taken from the library are to be returned within 15 days. Students/Staff should scan their ID card in the E-Gate Register scanner while entering the library.
  • Library users should possess their ID card while inside the library.
  • Books will be issued only on production of a bar-coded ID card.
  • All members must produce their ID card when the library staff demands it for checking.
  • Use of mobile phones is not allowed inside the library.
  • Complete silence is to be maintained inside the library. If conversation is necessary, it should be in a low tone only.
  • Personal belongings like files, folders, bags, jerkins, umbrellas, issued books, etc. are not allowed inside — they should be deposited at the security counter.
  • The library follows the Open Access System. Books/journals taken from the racks should be left on the tables — trained library assistants will replace them.
  • Students wearing shorts, pajamas, night dresses, round-neck or printed T-shirts are not allowed inside. Formal dress code applies.
  • The arrangement of chairs and furniture in the reading area should not be disturbed.
  • The librarian reserves the right to cancel the membership of any member found misbehaving or abusing library staff.

Books Issued & Returns

  • Books are to be returned within 15 days or on/before the date on the due-date slip.
  • Borrowers must ensure the book is in good condition with page numbers intact.
  • Staff/students must return books whenever called by the librarian.
  • If a book is overdue, another book will not be issued.
  • Reference books & non-book materials like CDs are issued for two days only.
  • The borrower is responsible for any loss caused to borrowed documents.
  • Library resources are national assets — all users must protect them against damage, mutilation and theft.
  • Users are allowed to photocopy required articles (not full books).
  • Digital library users must enroll in the entry register; downloading information is permitted.
  • Accessing unwanted/unnecessary information from the internet is strictly prohibited.

About NDLI Club

National Digital Library of India Club (NDLI Club) is an enabler of career progression for students, job seekers, researchers and learners. NDLI Clubs are set up in institutes and nodal bodies, and these Clubs conduct events to help students develop knowledge, skill and traits beyond the regular curriculum — essential for progression in their respective professional domain. Events may be physical or virtual; virtual events are web-based and conducted through the NDLI Club portal, organized at institute, city/district/state/regional or all-India level.

Development of knowledge, skill and traits needs access to varieties of learning resources. NDLI Club members can access a huge repository of free and open-access learning resources, from anywhere and at any time — via desktop, laptop, tablet or mobile — through the National Digital Library of India (NDLI) (ndl.iitkgp.ac.in). NDLI and NDLI Club are initiatives of the Ministry of Education (MoE), Government of India, through NMEICT — a National Mission project and part of the Digital India Mission.

NDLI provides content for all academic levels, all disciplines, and in most widely used Indian languages — a service for all of India to learn, share, grow and democratize education.
